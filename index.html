⚡ JUNE 29 CONTEXT — The Cycle Bottom Debate Begins: Bitcoin fell to $59,023 overnight on June 24–25 — its lowest level since October 10, 2024 — before recovering modestly toward $60,500–$61,800. Spot Bitcoin ETF outflows have now persisted for seven consecutive weeks, with total AUM shrinking from nearly $113 billion at the end of 2025 to just $77.5 billion. A prominent Chinese miner, Jiang Zhuoer, sparked debate by predicting BTC could fall another 30% to $42,000–$44,000 by late 2026, citing Strategy's market net asset value (mNAV) ratio dropping to 0.72 — approaching the 0.70 level last seen during the May 2022 bull-to-bear transition, which preceded a Bitcoin bottom roughly six months later. Strategy shares fell below $100 for the first time since March 2024. Compounding the gloom, the Ethereum Foundation announced it is cutting 54 positions — 20% of its workforce — and slashing its 2026 operating budget by 40%, part of a broader wave of crypto layoffs hitting Algorand, Gemini, Crypto.com, and Messari this year. The CLARITY Act, once seen as a near-certain 2026 catalyst, has had its passage odds cut to 50% by Galaxy Digital, with a key procedural vote expected within five weeks before Congress breaks for recess — a delay to fall would remove a major regulatory tailwind. Not all the news is bearish: BitMine now controls over 4% of total ETH supply, real-world asset tokenization crossed $20 billion on-chain, and some analysts argue the market is overly fixated on lagging mNAV signals rather than improving on-chain fundamentals.
📰 This Week's Big Stories
JUNE 23–29, 2026 · RANKED BY MARKET IMPACT
BTC Hits Lowest Level Since October 2024
CRITICAL
Bitcoin dropped to $59,023 overnight June 24–25 — a level not seen since October 10, 2024 — before recovering to roughly $60,500–$61,800. The decline triggered over $850 million in liquidations across crypto markets, with a separate report citing $1 billion+ in total liquidations and a 60/40 long/short split on a single day. The Binance liquidation heatmap flagged $72,280 as a key level to watch on any further downside. QCP Capital noted the options market isn't convinced a single catalyst will resolve the range — broader alignment of macro and crypto-specific factors will be needed for a sustained move either direction.
7th Straight Week of BTC ETF Outflows — AUM Down to $77.5B
HIGH IMPACT
US spot Bitcoin ETF outflows accelerated from $113.8M to $469M in a single 24-hour period, extending a streak now in its seventh consecutive negative week. Total assets under management have shrunk to $77.5 billion, down from nearly $113 billion at the end of 2025 — a roughly 31% decline. ETF issuers are reportedly being forced to sell physical bitcoin on the open market to meet redemptions, adding real supply pressure at a time of already-weak demand. This is the most sustained institutional outflow period since spot ETFs launched.
Chinese Miner: BTC Could Fall 30% to $42-44K by Late 2026
BEARISH CALL
Jiang Zhuoer, founder of mining pool BTC.top, published a widely-circulated forecast pointing to October 31, 2026 as a possible bear-market bottom near $44,016, with a wider range of $42,000–$44,000. His model centers on Strategy's mNAV ratio falling to 0.72 — close to the 0.70 low recorded in May 2022, which preceded Bitcoin's actual cycle bottom of $15,476 by roughly six months. Jiang is explicit that mNAV troughing does not mean Bitcoin has bottomed — historically the gap between the two events has been substantial. He frames the setup as supporting "long MSTR, short BTC" arbitrage trades rather than a near-term bullish or bearish directional call.
Ethereum Foundation Cuts 20% of Staff, 40% of Budget
STRUCTURAL
The Ethereum Foundation announced June 23 that it eliminated 54 positions — roughly 20% of its approximately 270-person workforce — and cut its 2026 operating budget by 40%. The organization is reorganizing into five domain-focused protocol clusters, moving away from its historical role as Ethereum's central development engine toward a narrower "protocol overseer" mandate. The restructuring targets reducing annual treasury spend from ~15% to ~5% by 2030 — a pace described as sustainable indefinitely. Nine senior figures, including former co-executive directors Tomasz Stańczak and Hsiao-Wei Wang, have left the EF since January 2026. This adds to a broader 2026 wave of crypto layoffs at Algorand (-25%), Gemini (-30%), and Crypto.com (-12%).
CLARITY Act Odds Cut to 50% — Critical Vote Within 5 Weeks
REGULATORY
Galaxy Digital reduced its probability estimate for CLARITY Act passage in 2026 down to 50%, citing unresolved policy differences and a crowded Senate calendar. A key procedural vote is expected within the next five weeks, after which Congress goes on recess — a delay past that window would likely push the bill to fall, removing a major catalyst the market had partly priced in. Stellar (XLM) and XRP, both viewed as primary beneficiaries of regulatory clarity, are particularly sensitive to this timeline given their payment-focused institutional theses.
BitMine Now Holds Over 4% of Total ETH Supply
BULLISH ETH
BitMine Immersion Technologies continued its aggressive ETH accumulation strategy, pushing its total holdings above 4% of all circulating Ethereum — one of the largest single-entity crypto positions ever assembled by a public company. This sustained buying continues even as ETH price weakness persists, reinforcing the institutional dip-buying pattern seen throughout June across BTC and ETH. The scale of BitMine's position raises long-term questions about supply concentration, but in the near term it represents one of the clearest "smart money still buying" signals in a week dominated by bearish headlines.
XLM Tests Critical $0.20 Support After May DTCC Rally
TECHNICAL
Stellar (XLM) pulled back to test the $0.20 support zone this week, driven primarily by sector rotation — capital flowing from altcoins into Bitcoin amid persistent market fear, plus a technical breakdown below $0.20 triggering stop-losses. CoinMarketCap's AI analysis notes the drop reflects market-wide de-risking rather than any negative XLM-specific catalyst — Citi reportedly continues to name Stellar a top-5 network for tokenized assets, and Circle is expanding its Cross-Chain Transfer Protocol to Stellar for native USDC transfers. A hold above $0.20 keeps the broader bullish structure (built on the May DTCC partnership) intact; a clean break opens room toward $0.185.
RWA Tokenization Crosses $20 Billion On-Chain
STRUCTURAL BULLISH
Real-world asset tokenization surpassed the $20 billion on-chain milestone this month, according to BlockchainReporter's Weekly Tokenization Roundup — a sign that institutional capital continues flowing into digital asset infrastructure even as spot prices struggle. This divergence between deteriorating price action and expanding institutional infrastructure build-out is one of the more interesting undercurrents of this bear phase: on-chain developer activity and tokenization volume are not declining in lockstep with price, suggesting the structural growth story remains intact beneath the surface-level pain.
