⚡ JUNE 22 CONTEXT — Warsh Redefines the Fed Playbook: Kevin Warsh chaired his first FOMC meeting on June 17 and the committee held rates steady at 3.5%–3.75% as expected — but the real story was how he dismantled the Fed's communication framework. He stripped out the long-standing dovish forward-guidance language entirely, and the updated dot plot showed 9 of 18 participants now expect at least one rate hike in 2026 — a sharp reversal from March's median projection of a cut. Warsh himself declined to submit a dot, explicitly choosing to reduce forward guidance and present "the facts" rather than a roadmap. Crypto reacted immediately: $122M in BTC and ETH liquidations as leveraged longs got caught wrong-footed, and BTC slid from ~$66K to as low as $62,500 before stabilizing. By the weekend, easing US-Iran tensions and the Strait of Hormuz reopening provided enough relief for a recovery back to $65,130. Markets are now adjusting to a Fed that offers far less predictability than under Powell — expect continued volatility around any Fed speaker comments this week.

Warsh Guts Fed Forward Guidance — Dot Plot Flips Hawkish
CRITICAL
New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting (June 17) held rates at 3.5%–3.75% as expected, but he dramatically shortened the policy statement and removed language signaling future rate cuts. The updated dot plot showed 9 of 18 participants now anticipate at least one 2026 rate hike — a sharp shift from March's median cut projection. Warsh submitted no dot of his own, calling the new format about presenting "the facts" rather than guiding expectations. Markets, accustomed to Powell-era forward guidance, were caught off-guard. CoinDesk: "Digital assets are attempting to decouple from a complex macro environment" following the shift.
$122M Liquidated as Crypto Wobbles Post-Fed
HIGH IMPACT
The FOMC decision triggered $122M in BTC and ETH liquidations within hours as leveraged longs were caught on the wrong side of the hawkish repricing. BTC fell to $62,498.60 by Friday morning, down 2.4% from Thursday, while ETH slid to $1,687.60, down 2.2%. The move was "less about the rate hold itself and more about the repricing of expectations" per CryptoTimes. A stronger dollar and higher-for-longer rate expectations pressured non-yielding assets like crypto, gold, and silver. Rate cuts are now seen as "all but off the table for 2026."
US-Iran Peace Deal Formally Signed — Hormuz Reopening
BULLISH RELIEF
Following the formal signing in Switzerland, easing US-Iran tensions and the opening of the Strait of Hormuz provided market relief into the weekend, helping crypto recover off Friday's lows. The peace deal stripped haven assets of urgency but also removed the geopolitical risk premium that had compounded the early-June crash. Combined with the Bank of Japan's historic rate hike to 1.0%, the macro backdrop is "attempting to decouple" from its most chaotic month since the cycle began, per CoinDesk's Crypto Week Ahead.
XLM: +20.7% in 7 Days, DTCC Catalyst Still Compounding
STRONG WEEK
Stellar (XLM) advanced strongly this week, named alongside INJ and UNI as a top performer after Bitcoin's broader rally following Japan's rate hike. XLM surged 10% in a single session amid the CoinDesk 20 index decline (June 18), and gained another leg after Centrifuge integrated yield-bearing real-world assets (deJTRSY, deJAAA) directly onto Stellar on June 20 — deepening DTCC-adjacent RWA utility. SushiSwap cross-chain swaps also went live June 20, boosting liquidity. Traders are split: a buy-signal targets $0.243 breakout with a $0.256 target (+13.6%), while bears note XLM remains "trapped in a broad descending structure" until it clears resistance decisively.
Morgan Stanley Files Spot ETH ETF With Built-In Staking
BULLISH ETH
Morgan Stanley filed an amended S-1 for a spot Ethereum ETF featuring built-in staking at a 0.14% management fee — directly undercutting BlackRock's existing offering on price. This intensifies the ETH ETF competition and could accelerate institutional inflows once approved. The timing is notable: ETH is trading 77%+ below its all-time high, and on-chain data shows 70%+ of supply currently in profit despite the price decline, with long-term holder accumulation rising even as price struggles.
BitMine Now Controls 4.66% of Total ETH Supply
BULLISH ETH
BitMine continued aggressively buying Ethereum despite ongoing price struggles, pushing its total holdings to 4.66% of the entire circulating ETH supply — one of the largest single-entity ETH positions ever assembled. This continues the institutional dip-buying pattern seen throughout June (Strategy, Bitmine, Strive on the BTC side). K33 Research separately reported that long-term BTC holders (2+ years) have seen exceptionally low reactivation in 2026 — only 218,421 BTC moved by June 6, far below recent years, indicating strong hands are not selling into this dip.
Strategy Adds Another 1,587 BTC Amid Growing STRC Scrutiny
MIXED SIGNAL
Strategy added 1,587 BTC to its treasury this week, continuing its post-sale reaccumulation pattern. However, scrutiny around its STRC preferred stock dividend financing structure is growing — the same mechanism that triggered the symbolic 32 BTC sale three weeks ago. The market is watching closely whether Strategy can sustain its buying pace without further dividend-driven sales. Separately, Cardano's Hoskinson defended a disputed 1,096 BTC allocation as an internal audit dispute heats up within the Cardano community — an unrelated but parallel governance flashpoint.
Ethereum Glamsterdam Enters Final Devnet Testing
STRUCTURAL
Ethereum's Glamsterdam upgrade entered final devnet testing this week with all planned EIPs now running, including ePBS (enshrined proposer-builder separation) and a potential gas limit increase to 200 million. The next milestone is public testnet deployment. CryptoTimes warns that any setbacks in testing could push mainnet activation deeper into H2 2026, compounding narrative pressure on ETH at a time when funding concerns and price weakness are already creating investor anxiety. A clean testnet run would be a meaningful sentiment catalyst for ETH bulls.
