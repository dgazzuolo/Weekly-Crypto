BTC Crashes to 2026 Low $59,100 on Jobs Data + ETF Exodus
CRITICAL
Bitcoin crashed to a 2026 low of $59,100 after the strongest US jobs report in 18 months revived rate-hike expectations. Over $1.7 billion in crypto positions were liquidated, and a record 13-day Bitcoin ETF outflow streak reached $4.33 billion. The Fear & Greed Index hit 12 (Extreme Fear) on June 3 — the lowest since the FTX collapse era. BTC is now -42% from its $109K January ATH. Technically, BTC entered the breakdown stage of a bear flag with a measured target near $50K, though the 200-week SMA near $60K is a strong historical support.
Strategy Buys Back 1,550 BTC — Reaffirms Accumulation Model
BULLISH
Strategy bought 1,550 bitcoin for approximately $101 million at an average price of $65,332, bringing total holdings to 845,256 BTC, just one week after its symbolic $2.5M sale. The buyback directly countered the bearish narrative that Saylor was abandoning accumulation. Strategy also raised $181 million through stock sales, funding further BTC purchases. The pattern mirrors December 2022: sell small → buy big → bottom signal. MVRV Z-Score approaching historical cycle-low zones reinforces this thesis.
Bitmine Buys 126,971 ETH — Biggest 2026 Purchase Despite Crash
BULLISH ETH
Bitmine bought 126,971 ETH last week, worth roughly $214 million at current prices — its biggest ETH purchase of 2026 — despite chairman Tom Lee's earlier calls to slow purchases. ETH RSI reached 21.8 last week — extreme oversold territory. This represents one of the largest single-entity ETH accumulation events of the cycle. Combined with whales buying $322M ETH the prior week, institutional smart money is aggressively accumulating on the dip while retail sentiment is at Extreme Fear.
SpaceX IPO Roadshow Opens — June 12 Nasdaq Listing
HIGH IMPACT
SpaceX's roadshow opened June 4, with pricing expected June 11 and Nasdaq trading targeting June 12. The $75 billion capital raise is drawing retail and institutional liquidity away from crypto into equities. Buried in the filing: SpaceX holds 18,712 BTC worth approximately $1.29 billion — one of the largest known corporate Bitcoin holders. Analysts warn of "exit liquidity rotation" — investors selling BTC to fund exposure to SpaceX IPO shares. Long-term BTC bullish (SpaceX = BTC endorsement); short-term bearish (capital rotation).
CLARITY Act: Garlinghouse vs Dimon — Senate Floor Vote Pending
HIGH IMPACT
The CLARITY Act Senate floor vote remains the defining regulatory event of 2026. The White House is targeting a July 4 deadline for the bill to become law. Polymarket puts 2026 passage odds at 59%. Garlinghouse vs Dimon: The CLARITY Act fight nobody's watching — Ripple's CEO and JPMorgan's CEO are publicly clashing over the bill's bank-stablecoin provisions. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh — First Meeting June 16–17
MACRO
New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh chairs his first policy meeting on June 16–17. That meeting is the next major catalyst. Until then, the $60,000 to $65,000 range defines the trade for Bitcoin. The strong jobs report (strongest in 18 months) has markets pricing in a possible rate hike — which would be highly bearish for crypto. If Warsh signals a pause, expect significant relief rally. Markets are now pricing ~45% odds of a hike at this meeting, up from near-zero two weeks ago.
Bybit Launches Tokenized US Stock IPOs — SpaceX, Anthropic
STRUCTURAL
Bybit challenges Wall Street with a massive push into tokenized US stock IPOs — retail investors can now buy shares at official underwritten prices through the crypto exchange, bypassing Wall Street's exclusive pre-IPO clubs. SpaceX (SPCX) and Anthropic are the two flagship offerings. Crypto's recovery remains elusive as SpaceX and Anthropic IPOs loom. This is a significant structural development: crypto exchanges are becoming full-service capital markets venues, directly competing with traditional brokerages for IPO allocations.
