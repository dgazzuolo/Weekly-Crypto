US-Iran Peace Deal Signed — Strait of Hormuz to Reopen
CRITICAL BULLISH
Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif announced the deal June 14: "The Peace Deal between the United States of America and Islamic Republic of Iran has been REACHED. Both sides have declared the immediate and permanent termination of military operations." President Trump ordered the removal of the US naval blockade of Iranian ports. The Strait of Hormuz — through which 20% of global oil passes — will reopen once the formal signing happens in Switzerland on June 19. Oil dropped 3%+. Crypto surged across the board. BTC reclaimed $65K, XRP led alts with +8.8%. This removes the single biggest macro risk that drove the June crash.
SpaceX IPO: $75B Raise, $178 on Debut — Holds 18,712 BTC
HIGH IMPACT
SpaceX priced its IPO at $135/share on June 11, raising $75 billion in the largest IPO in history, then debuted on Nasdaq June 12. As of June 15 it trades at $177.99 — up 32% from IPO price in 3 days. The filing confirmed SpaceX holds 18,712 BTC (~$1.29B at current prices), one of the largest corporate BTC treasuries. The capital rotation that contributed to $5.75B in BTC ETF outflows appears to be resolving — with SpaceX IPO now priced and trading, that overhang is clearing. Bybit's tokenized SPCX shares via xStocks allowed retail access at IPO price.
SpaceX IPO Chaos Makes Hyperliquid Look "Superior" — Grayscale
BULLISH HYPE
Centralized IPO access issues during the SpaceX listing exposed failures in traditional tokenized-stock platforms. Investor Simon Dedic called Hyperliquid's on-chain perps the "superior vehicle" for SpaceX, Anthropic, and OpenAI exposure. Grayscale's formal research note revealed HIP-3 perpetual markets hit $3.2B in open interest in June 2026, with $200B+ traded since launch. The first S&P 500 perpetual futures product launched on Hyperliquid in March 2026 via an S&P Dow Jones Indices license. Separately, Kalshi launched CFTC-regulated HYPE perpetual futures on June 12 — the first US-regulated derivatives on HYPE, a milestone for institutional access.
Galaxy Research: Only 4 of 13 Bottom Signals Triggered
BEARISH CAUTION
Galaxy Research published a sobering analysis: historically, Bitcoin bottoms require 10–13 of their tracked signals to trigger simultaneously. As of this week, only 4 have fired. Their base case places a possible BTC floor between $40,000 and $46,000 by late 2026. Signals still missing: sustained ETF inflow reversal, MVRV Z-Score near-zero, miner capitulation completion (difficulty dropped 10% but needs more), and options market structure shift. The Iran deal provides relief, but Galaxy warns "traders still need stronger fund flows before calling a durable recovery."
CLARITY Act Won't Meet July 4 Deadline — Two-Month Window
DELAYED
The White House's self-imposed July 4 target for the CLARITY Act to become law will not be met. The bill has a "two-month window" to pass per analysts. Garlinghouse vs Dimon: Ripple's CEO and JPMorgan's CEO are publicly battling over the bank-stablecoin provisions — Dimon's opposition is the primary holdup. Polymarket: 59% 2026 passage odds. Despite the delay, Dedic called it "the single biggest catalyst this industry has ever had" once passed. New timeline: Senate floor vote likely late June/July, full passage August–September at earliest.
ETF Outflows: $5.75B Total — Cash-and-Carry Unwind, Not Panic
STRUCTURAL
Whalesbook analysis reframes the $5.75B outflow story: the dominant driver was unwinding of "cash-and-carry" arbitrage trades (basis trading), not retail panic or institutional exits. As futures premiums compressed, arbitrageurs simultaneously sold ETF positions and bought spot — creating artificial outflow signals. The SpaceX IPO capital rotation was secondary. This reframing is bullish: when the unwind completes, the structural demand from real long-only ETF holders reasserts. Watch for net inflows to resume once futures premium rebuilds.
Mining Difficulty Drops 10% — Historical Bottom Signal
BOTTOM WATCH
Bitcoin mining difficulty dropped over 10% on June 14 — one of the sharpest drops of this cycle. Historically, major difficulty drops signal miner capitulation: the weakest miners are forced offline by unprofitability, reducing hash rate and triggering an automatic difficulty reset. In 2018 and 2022, major difficulty drops occurred within weeks of cycle bottoms. Combined with MVRV Z-Score approaching historical lows and Strategy's counter-cyclical buyback (1,550 BTC), this is the strongest cluster of bottom signals seen this cycle — though Galaxy cautions 9 more signals are needed.
Fed Chair Warsh First Decision Wednesday — 98.6% Hold Odds
MACRO WATCH
Fed Chair Kevin Warsh delivers his first FOMC decision on June 17 with new projections. CME FedWatch: 98.6% probability of no rate change. The Iran peace deal and oil price drop materially reduces near-term inflation risk, making a hold essentially certain. The real market focus is on the dot plot and Warsh's tone: will he maintain Powell's easing bias or signal a hawkish pivot? A dovish hold with softened language = crypto positive. A hawkish hold with upward rate revisions = crypto negative. BTC at $66,500 is pricing in a benign outcome — any surprise will move markets sharply.
